Elizaveta Romantsova

Makievka, 19 y.o., orphan

"About their history, Elizaveta explains: In 2014, when I was 15, my sister Valeria and our mother, and I had to leave our home in city of Makievka, Donetsk region because of war in Eastern Ukraine. Our brother Oleksandr could not leave with us because at the age of 16 he did not make passport. One year later, making passport was impossible. He stayed at home with our father (Our parents were divorced since 2004).After we left Makiivka, we spent three months living in Chernivetskiy region with our grandmother. In September we were accepted into trade school in city of Khmelnytskiy. Our profession was cook and confectioner. We were living in dormitory. Our mother could not find place to live in Khmelnytskiy. She had to live and work in the village, that is why we did not see her for half of a year. Six month later she found a place in dormitory, but we lived apart for two years, because we were not given room to live in that dormitory. At third year of our studying mother succeed getting us a room and we lived together during that year.Due to financial difficulties in family, my sister and I went working at the end of our education. It was hard to work and study in the same time, so our marks got worse.We finished trade school in june 2017. A month later our mother went abroad to earn money for our living. This month we learned about “Hebron”, and decided to seize the opportunity for self-development and realizing our potential in life.We are grateful to God for people in this organization who serve at His will."
